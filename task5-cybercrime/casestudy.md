UPI [online payment fraud]

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime in India where attackers trick users into authorizing fake 
transactions. It often exploits lack of awareness and urgency.

A typical scenario begins when the victim receives a phone call or message from someone pretending to be a bank official, delivery agent, or customer support executive. The fraudster claims that there is an issue with the victim’s account or it offers a reward/refund. Step-by-step, the attackers ask the victim to click on a payment link or send a collect request via a UPI app. The victim is instructed to enter their UPI PIN, believing that they are receiving money. In reality, entering of the PIN authorizes a payment, transfering money to the scammer account. In some cases, scammer also ask user to install screen-sharing apps, gaining access to sensitive information.

Targets are usually common smartphone users, especially those who are unfamiliar with digital payments, elderly individuals, or people in urgent situations.

The consequences include immediate financial loss, emotional stress, and in some cases, identity misuse. Recovery of money is difficult once the transaction is complete.

A well-known pattern of such scams has been reported multiple times by Reserve Bank of India (RBI) warnings, which advise users never to share their UPI PIN or approve unknown payment requests.

Awareness, careful verification, and never sharing confidential details are key to preventing such fraud.
